Behind the Music – Featherfall

A meditation on healing, letting go, and transcending the weight we carry

Featherfall was born during a time when I almost walked away from the piano completely. Years of rigid theory and structure had drained the joy from something I once loved deeply. I felt burnt out—creatively, emotionally, and spiritually. But when I let go of the rules and began to play by ear and feeling, something shifted. I reconnected with music in its purest form. That’s when Featherfall emerged—effortlessly, intuitively.

It marked the beginning of my journey as a self-taught composer, where melodies came not from sheet music, but from emotion and instinct.

At its core, Featherfall is a reflection of that journey. It represents letting go—of pressure, expectations, the need to “do it right”, and the fear of not being good enough. It’s about surrendering control and trusting softness. Floating, not falling.

The music follows this transformation. It starts with a solitary piano—tender and personal—and gradually unfolds into something larger: rising strings and layered choir, building in volume and emotion. The piece ends in a crescendo of sound, symbolizing growth, release, and rebirth.

“Floating, not falling. Releasing, not breaking.”

The Cover Art

The starry night sky reflects the vastness of our inner world—our thoughts and emotions. The feather represents the self—falling not with heaviness, but with grace. It symbolizes the gentle release of past burdens, the long-carried weight finally set free.

As the feather dissolves into stardust, it becomes one with the universe—representing transformation, and ultimately, the soul’s return to peace. Much like reaching a state of nirvana: release from suffering, a union with something eternal.

The silhouette of the woman watches all this unfold. She is the human behind the metaphor—looking up at her own transformation with awe and quiet reflection. The meditative sound of the piano mirrors this moment—soft, spiritual, and deeply personal.
