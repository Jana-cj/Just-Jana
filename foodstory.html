Healing Through Food


Posted: October 02, 2025


When Food Felt Like the Enemy

There was a time when food felt more like a burden than a blessing. I remember even saying to my mum once, “everything in this world would be good if there was no food".

As a child, I had almost no appetite. I would push food around my plate for hours, fall asleep mid-bite, live with constant stomach pains and sometimes even flush food down the toilet because I was too scared to come home with leftovers. My mom would often tell others that I just move food around my plate without actually eating it. It got to a point where she even had my horoscope checked, and they told her I had apala (bad luck) until I turned 13 - that during those years I would remain extremely skinny. But the truth was, I stayed skinny long after that, because I never felt peaceful enough to enjoy meals. Most dinner times were noisy, chaotic, and stressful. In that atmosphere, food never stood a chance.

And honestly, the food itself didn’t help. Take chickpeas for example - I hated them. Growing up, they were always just boiled with barely any flavor, so bland that I made a promise to myself as a kid, “When I become an adult, I’m never eating chickpeas again”. But little did I know I’d end up breaking that promise eventually...

The Pain I Couldn’t Explain

“Listen to your body-it always whispers before it screams.”

Most mornings started the same: breakfast, a glass of dairy milk, and then a rush upstairs to be sick. Vomiting and stomach aches became so normal that I didn’t even question them. Once, the pain was so bad I was taken to hospital, only to be told to drink more water.

The Realisation: Food Has Faces

“Once you see, you can’t unsee.”

One memory that stayed with me since childhood was at a farm café in Sri Lanka. I saw “rabbit” on the menu and, innocently, asked if we could get one, thinking it meant a pet. My dad explained it was for eating, which left me in complete shock. My sister then added, “you already eat animals: beef is cow, chicken is hen, pork is pig…”

That moment opened my eyes. I couldn’t stop thinking about it.
Years later, in 2013, while my family and I were having dinner, I saw on the news that a monk had set himself on fire in protest of the meat industry. The image plastered itself in my mind (he was walking around in flames, and it looked like something out of a movie). Sitting there with a bowl of beef noodles in front of me, I felt extreme guilt and sadness. His act of sacrifice carried meaning, and it stirred something deep in me that never quite went away.

The Turning Point

“When your heart breaks open, it also opens wider.”

At 18, I saw slaughterhouse footage for the first time - a cow being tortured before her throat was slit. I couldn’t sleep that night. I promised myself I would never eat animals again.

Around that time, my partner had also discovered Phillip Wollen’s debate, “Animals Should Be Off the Menu”. Together, we started our vegetarian journey.

Eventually, we uncovered the hidden cruelty behind the dairy and egg industries:

  • Dairy: cows are kept in a cycle of forced pregnancies by being artifically inseminated, their calves taken away within hours of birth, daughters destined for the same cycle, and sons often killed weeks from being born for veal. The milk meant for the calf is taken for human use, leaving mothers crying out for days. When their bodies are no longer able to produce enough milk, these mothers too are sent off to slaughter for meat.
  • Eggs: male chicks, deemed “useless” because they can’t lay eggs, are killed within hours - ground alive or suffocated. The hens who remain are confined and pushed beyond their natural limits until their bodies give out, at which point they are no longer seen as “useful” and are killed for meat.

Learning this made me realise that even as a vegetarian, I was still objectifying animals - treating their bodies and their babies as products for my use. And I wanted no part in that.

Once I saw it, there was no unseeing. Compassion couldn’t stop halfway. Up until that point, I truly thought vegetarianism was it. I didn’t even know what vegan meant. I honestly thought it was just a shorter and fancier word for vegetarian 😅.
But that was when I understood vegetarianism wasn’t the end - it was just the beginning of a deeper journey.

Almost immediately, everything shifted. Switching from dairy milk to plant milk stopped the vomiting. My stomach pains disappeared, my skin cleared, and even my singing voice felt freer.

Later, a medical test confirmed what my body had been trying to tell me all along: I was lactose intolerant. Suddenly, years of unexplained pain made sense. It wasn’t only an ethical choice anymore - it was also my body’s path to healing.

From Vegetarian to Vegan

“When knowledge meets compassion, change becomes effortless.”

In 2019, when my partner and I moved to Australia, we transitioned to veganism. At first, we leaned towards plant-based convenience foods like vegan schnitzels, sausages, and patties (classic student meals). But when COVID hit in 2020, we had the time and space to dive deeper.

We leaned into whole-food, plant-based living- whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, a variety of potatoes and fresh produce (fruits and vegetables)- simple, nourishing, and transformative.

Food Became Healing

“The body heals when we feed it love, not just calories.”

As I trained and ate more whole foods, my body changed. I built 10–12 kg of muscle in a year, my skin glowed, my nails strengthened, my hair grew a lot faster, and I stopped breaking out completely. More than that, I had energy and I felt happier, lighter, and more alive. Food was no longer the enemy - it was the very thing fueling my healing.

Rediscovering the Kitchen

“Cooking is just another way of creating art-with flavors, colors, and love.”

Going vegan also pushed us to experiment in the kitchen. We explored new spices, herbs, and recipes we never would’ve tried before. Our taste buds changed - we stopped craving processed food, and when we occasionally did eat it out of convenience, we noticed the dip in our mood and energy right away.

Ironically, this adventure brought chickpeas back into my life in the best way possible. With the right mix of spices and creativity, they went from the food I swore I’d never touch again to one of my favorite curries. And of course, my partner had a hand in that. He just has this uncanny talent for making things I once hated completely lovable - first my curly hair, then my skin tone, and now even chickpeas. (I can’t help but wonder what’s next 😂).

The kitchen became a place of joy, not dread. A place to create, to experiment, to connect.

Six Years Later

“Alignment feels like peace.”

It’s been six years since going vegan, and I’ve never felt more aligned - physically, mentally, and spiritually. What started as a personal journey of alignment grew into something much bigger. This path opened our eyes to question conventions, to live with more awareness, and to align our actions with our values.

If you’re curious, I gently invite you to notice how food makes you feel. Explore, experiment, and see what shifts.

Because for me, food is no longer the enemy - it’s love, healing, and connection on a plate.
Looking back, I can hardly believe I ever said I wished food didn’t exist - because now, I truly live to eat. Food means everything to me.

✨ Lesson Learned

Sometimes it’s worth questioning the “usual way” and experimenting for yourself, otherwise, you may never discover what truly works for your body, mind, heart and soul.
