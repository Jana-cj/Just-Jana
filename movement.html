Importance of Movement





The Importance of Movement

Regular movement supports heart, brain, mood, sleep, and longevity. A practical target is 150–300 min/week moderate (or 75–150 min vigorous) plus 2+ strength sessions, while sprinkling light movement through your day. See references.

  • Lower risk of CVD, diabetes, and several cancers
  • Improved mood, focus, and sleep quality
  • Better bone density, balance, and energy (hello NEAT!)

Different kinds of movement

Swipe on mobile • Drag or use dots

Aerobic (Cardio)

Walk, jog, cycle, dance. Build endurance; sprinkle intervals when short on time.

20–40 min • 3–5×/wk

Strength

Push, pull, hinge, squat, carry. Weights, bands, or bodyweight for resilience.

2–3×/wk

Mobility & Flexibility

Gentle ROM and stretches; great post-training or on easy days.

Most days • 5–10 min

NEAT

Incidental movement: stairs, walking calls, chores. Small moves, big impact.

All day

Balance & Coordination

Single-leg stands, carries, agility. Keeps ankles, hips & brain sharp.

2–3×/wk

Mind–Body

Yoga, tai chi, breath work. Down-shift the nervous system and restore.

Any day • 5–20 min

The movement pyramid

Build from the base up.

NEAT & light activity (daily)Walking, stairs, posture resets
Cardio (3–5×/wk)Moderate steady; optional intervals
Strength (2–3×/wk)Push • Pull • Hinge • Squat • Carry
Mobility, balance & skillDaily sprinkles

Body × Mind balance

  • Up-shift: intervals, heavier sets, skill practice
  • Down-shift: 2–5 min nasal breathing, slow walk, gentle mobility
  • Signals: Poor sleep/mood or low desire to train 3+ days → back off

Why this?

If your body is tired but your mind is restless, lean into creative outlets (journaling, music practice, sketching, gentle flow) that stretch imagination without taxing the body. If your mind is tired but your body is idle, choose movement that restores energy and presence (walk, light cycle, mobility).

Movement @ work (doable)

  • 3 per hour: stand up, shoulder rolls, 30s calf pumps
  • Walking calls: headset + lap the corridor/outdoors
  • Drink & drift: smaller water = more micro-walks
  • Stairs policy: 1–2 flights whenever possible
  • Desk band: 1× pull-aparts/rows between tasks
  • Lunch 10: 10-minute brisk walk before eating

Prolonged sitting raises cardiometabolic risk; frequent light breaks help. Refs.
