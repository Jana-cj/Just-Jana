Fragile Beginnings

Growing up, I wasn’t a strong kid — in fact, I was extremely skinny. Not by choice, but because most foods didn’t sit well with me. It was my body’s response to stress, allergies, and environments I couldn’t control. But one thing brought me to life: netball.

Discovering Netball — My First Safe Space

At eight years old, I joined the school netball team. It was the first space I wasn’t compared to my sister. The court became my escape — where I felt fast, present, and powerful. Nothing made me feel more myself than netball.

Success Met with Doubt

I was named netball captain, but instead of joy, my mother feared others would think she influenced it. It planted the seed of imposter syndrome. Even when I made it into the newspaper, others assumed it was my sister — I had become invisible.

Lessons in Resilience

Criticism followed me: “lucky shots,” “why is she even here?” But I learned — people project their insecurities. You can’t control their words, but you can choose not to believe them. Their opinion is not your truth.

A New Chapter in Australia

In 2019, I moved to Australia. For the first time, I explored who I was — not who I was told to be. My partner encouraged me to step into the gym. I weighed 43kg, but I began training — not to look a certain way, but to feel strong.

Falling in Love with Movement

I built muscle, gained healthy weight, and felt clarity like never before. Movement became my anchor. I joined my university’s netball team, ran my first 10km, and discovered peace in the process.

The Injury That Changed Everything

In 2022, I broke my femur during a netball final. Relearning to walk was humbling. It taught me something I never understood before: movement is a privilege. I would never take it for granted again.

Rising Stronger

While healing, I trained my upper body with calisthenics — pull-ups, dips, push-ups. I returned to running and built a stronger body and mindset. This wasn’t just recovery. It was a rebirth.

Why I Created This Space

As South Asian women, we’re taught to shrink. But strength is not just in muscles — it’s in healing, self-respect, and choosing growth. This space is for every girl who’s been overlooked. It’s time we reclaimed our power.

My Message to You

If you’ve ever felt not good enough, know this: you are. Your worth isn’t defined by others. Movement saved me — and it can do the same for you. I’m here to walk beside you, every step of the way.
